1. Wildlife and Ecosystems

Savanna and Grasslands

The savanna and grassland regions of India, such as the Bandipur and Jim Corbett National Parks in Karnataka, are known for their diverse wildlife. These areas support species like the Indian tiger, Asiatic lion, and various herbivores.

Protected Areas

India boasts numerous wildlife sanctuaries and national parks dedicated to the conservation of various species. Notable examples include:

  • Ranthambore National Park: Famous for its population of Bengal tigers and rich biodiversity.
  • Kaziranga National Park: Renowned for its one-horned rhinoceroses and diverse bird species.

Wetlands and Mangroves

India's wetlands and mangrove forests are crucial for maintaining ecological balance. The Sundarbans in West Bengal, for example, are the world's largest tidal halophytic mangrove forest and are home to the Bengal tiger.

Forests and Mountain Ecosystems

The country's forests, including the Western Ghats and the Himalayan region, offer a range of ecosystems from tropical rainforests to temperate woodlands. These areas are habitats for species like the snow leopard, red panda, and various endemic flora.
